# KisanVaani — MP Q&A battlecard

Keep each answer under 25 seconds. Lead with the direct answer, then one proof point.

## 1. Who asked for this problem?

The official Code for Communities page says every challenge came from an MP’s office. Shri Lavu Sri Krishna Devarayalu specifically says Kisan Alert was drawn from farmer needs in Narasaraopet. The initiative lists six supporting MPs, four constituency-led challenges and a pathway to pilot selected solutions with the sponsoring offices.

## 2. How is this different from Kisan Call Centre or Kisan Sarathi?

It is complementary, not a replacement. Kisan Sarathi already provides multi-channel, multilingual advisory, expert routing and national-scale public infrastructure. KisanVaani is a configurable constituency deployment layer: natural-language intake on any phone, four-source grounding, confidence and risk gates, structured expert handoff, and clustering of repeated local queries into officer-verified alerts.

## 3. What have you actually built?

A deployed product with voice and SMS flows, a 12-language farmer interface, agronomy rules for 14 crops, four data connectors, expert-escalation logic, query logs, outbreak clustering and a working command centre. The live proof is [kisan-vaani.vercel.app](https://kisan-vaani.vercel.app).

## 4. Is the dashboard showing real farmer outcomes?

No. The displayed rows are clearly labelled demonstration data. We will not convert prototype activity into impact claims. A real pilot must generate the reach, resolution and cost evidence.

## 5. How do you stop AI hallucinations from harming farmers?

The answer is grounded in crop, location, soil, weather, market and agronomy inputs. The system applies risk and confidence gates; low-confidence or high-risk cases are escalated to a human expert. AI handles volume—RSK, KVK or KCC retains authority.

## 6. What exactly do you need from an MP’s office?

One pilot block, one priority crop cycle, a permitted farmer roster with consent, an official IVR/SMS channel, one nodal agriculture officer, KVK/RSK participation and a place on the district review agenda. We bring the product, Telugu onboarding, local configuration, safety process, weekly dashboard and reporting.

## 7. Why start with one block?

Because adoption, crop patterns, language, expert capacity and telecom costs vary locally. One block makes the result attributable and auditable before public money or operational effort is committed at scale.

## 8. What will success look like?

We will publish six measures: reach and repeat use, time to useful advice, time to expert resolution, cluster precision and false positives, alerts acted on within 48 hours, and cost per useful action. Baselines and targets are agreed before launch; we do not invent them on stage.

## 9. What about farmer privacy and consent?

The pilot should collect only what is needed for advice and routing, with explicit consent, role-based access and a written retention/deletion policy agreed with the public partner before onboarding. Do not claim completed government compliance review unless it has actually occurred.

## 10. What is the cost?

The software is deployed, but the per-farmer operating cost depends on the official voice/SMS channel, call duration, usage and expert workload. The defensible answer is to measure cost per useful action in the block pilot after receiving a telecom quote—not announce a fictional national budget.

## 11. Can this scale nationally?

The pathway exists, but it must be earned. The 29 June 2026 PIB release reports 2.95 crore farmers, 730+ KVKs, 768 districts and 4,767 ICAR scientists connected through Kisan Sarathi. We are not claiming a current integration; a successful block pilot creates the evidence to seek one.

## Red lines — never claim on stage

- Do not claim yield saved, income increased, pesticide reduced or outbreaks prevented without field results.
- Do not quote the reference deck’s ₹12-per-farmer, ₹5-lakh-pilot, 25:1-return or 10–12% yield figures as validated KisanVaani outcomes.
- Do not present seeded dashboard rows as farmers served.
- Do not claim a live Indian toll-free number, DLT approval, government registry access or official KCC/KVK/RSK integration.
- Do not claim live ground sensors or IMD/WINDS integration.
- Do not quote unsupported smartphone/feature-phone percentages.
- Say **12 farmer-interface languages**, not 22 shipped languages.
